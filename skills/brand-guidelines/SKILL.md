### Colour Usage Hierarchy

1. Always **lead with oOh! Orange** — it is the singular primary brand colour
2. Use **Standard White** as the natural partner (most slides/pages)
3. Use **Urban Grey** for subtle backgrounds and section dividers
4. Use **Moonlight Purple** for key statement slides and premium moments
5. Bring in **Twilight Purple** only for infographics, data viz, or executive presentations
6. Secondary palette (Ocean Blue, Coral Peach, Sky Blue, Desert Yellow) supports but never leads

**Purples — when to use:**
- ✅ Backgrounds/accents where they enhance contrast
- ✅ Infographics and data visualisation (contrast/highlighting)
- ✅ Premium/elevated moments — events, executive presentations, reports
- ❌ Standard brand collateral (trade ads, campaigns)
- ❌ Never in a way that dilutes or overshadows oOh! Orange

### PPT Colour Weight Variants

| Variant               | When to use                                             |
|-----------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| Touch of Orange       | Detailed reports, text-heavy corporate documents (white dominant, orange accents only) |
| Mid-weight Orange     | Standard brand presentations, proposals (balanced orange panels + white content) |
| Majority Orange       | Cover slides, section breakers, events, pull-up banners (bold orange throughout) |

**Universal PPT rules:**
- Cover slides are always orange — regardless of deck weight
- Key Statement slide is always Moonlight Purple background + white text
- White is the dominant background even in mid-weight and bespoke decks
- oOh! logo always present, bottom-right, every slide without exception
- No decorative lines under titles — use whitespace or background colour instead

## Typography

### The Only Font: Century Gothic

Century Gothic is a geometric sans-serif system font — versatile, clean, high legibility at small sizes.

```css
font-family: 'Century Gothic', CenturyGothic, AppleGothic, sans-serif;
```

| Weight     | Availability          | Usage                                               |
|------------|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Regular    | All staff             | Headings, body copy, footnotes, general text        |
| Bold       | All staff             | Subheadings, key statements, section breakers, CTAs |
| Light      | Marketing/design only | Decorative/special use                              |
| Extra Bold | Marketing/design only | Decorative/special use                              |
| Outlined   | Marketing/design only | Decorative/special use                              |

### Typography Hierarchy (PPT)

| Element                | Font            | Size       | Notes                              |
|------------------------|-----------------|------------|------------------------------------|
| Headings               | CG Regular      | 24pt       | Tracking: normal                   |
| Small headings         | CG Regular      | 16pt       | Tracking: normal                   |
| Body copy              | CG Regular      | 12pt       | Tracking: normal                   |
| Section number         | CG Regular/Bold | 60pt       | Large number on section slides     |
| Section title          | CG Regular/Bold | 44pt       | Below the section number           |
| Key statement slide    | CG Regular/Bold | 36pt       | Tracking: normal                   |
| Big stats/metrics      | CG Bold         | 48–72pt    | Orange colour on white background  |
| Footnotes              | CG Regular      | min 9pt    | Leading = point size + 4           |
| Links                  | CG Regular      | —          | Colour: oOh! Orange                |
| Button text            | CG Bold         | —          | Sentence case, centred, no wrapping|

### Typography Rules

- **Sentence case always** — no title case, no ALL CAPS, no all-lower-case
- No underline on text
- No highlight/colour behind text
- No drop shadow on or behind text
- No strikethrough
- No equalising character height
- Italic only sparingly and purposefully (very limited use)
- One typeface only — Century Gothic. No mixing with other fonts.

### CTA Buttons

- Background: oOh! Orange (primary), or Moonlight Purple/white as alternates
- Text: Century Gothic Bold, sentence case, centred
- Corner radius: 5px (softened, not round)
- Height: 50px
- Do not wrap text — single line only

## Data Visualisation

For charts/graphs, always use brand colours with a monochromatic approach within each chart:

- **Primary data series:** oOh! Orange shades (dominant)
- **Secondary data series:** Moonlight Purple shades → Twilight Purple shades
- **Chart backgrounds:** Urban Grey (light) OR Moonlight Purple (dark) — never white alone

**Infographic colour priority:**
1. oOh! Orange (dominant — icons, highlights, step markers)
2. Standard White (backgrounds, text on dark)
3. Moonlight Purple (funnels, dark backgrounds)
4. Twilight Purple (segments, secondary data)
5. Coral Peach / Sky Blue (very selective — softness only)
6. Urban Grey (inactive states, remaining segments)

**Chart typography:**
- Data labels (bar charts): Century Gothic Bold, min 10pt
- Data labels (pie charts): Century Gothic Regular 18pt, min 10pt
- Legend text: Century Gothic Regular, min 8pt

**Chart style rules:**
- No gridlines, no chart borders — data floats cleanly
- Bar graphs on white: monochromatic orange bars in varying shades/opacity
- Bar graphs on dark: Moonlight Purple background, Twilight Purple bar shades
- Donut charts: orange segment + Urban Grey remaining; or Twilight Purple tint + Urban Grey
- Percentage figures on donuts: CG Regular 18pt white on coloured segments

## Tone of Voice

The oOh! tone is: **Self-assured · Good-natured · Go-getting · Trust-worthy**

| Tone pillar    | In practice                                                                                          |
|----------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Good-natured   | Friendly and approachable. Straightforward language. No qualifiers ("quite", "fairly", "a bit", "one of"). Actions speak louder than words. |
| Self-assured   | Confident, not arrogant. Inclusive language. Clear and succinct. Minimal acronyms and jargon.       |
| Go-getting     | Energetic and optimistic. Repeat brand messages consistently. Excite people about the future.       |
| Trust-worthy   | Honest and direct. Drop the fluff — get to the point. No flowery language. Use explainers and myth-busters. |

## Grammar Rules

- **Company name:** "oOh!media" on first mention, then "oOh!" subsequently
- **Tagline:** "Unmissable" — sentence case
- **Pronouns:** Use "we, us, our" to humanise; be mindful of gendered pronouns
- **No Oxford comma**
- **Sentence case** for all headings and titles — no title case, no ALL CAPS
- Use hyphens and en dashes correctly
- Avoid qualifiers: "one of", "quite", "fairly", "a bit", "a little"
- **PPT writing:** Short, impactful headlines (not full sentences); bullets over paragraphs; one idea per slide; visual over words

## Photography Guidelines

### Asset Photography (oOh! owned)

- Only use professionally taken and marketing-approved photography
- Engaging, bright, premium quality imagery
- Capture during day/late afternoon for vibrant energy
- Incorporate orange in lights, clothing, sunsets where possible
- Editing: remove graffiti, crop distracting elements, enhance brightness, ensure skies enhance the image, bring out orange tones
- Never place gradients, text, or colour boxes over assets without marketing approval

### Stock Photography

- High quality, professional stock imagery aligned with premium brand nature
- Use audience images, location, seasonal, or abstract imagery
- Natural lighting, rich colours, bright/bold/crisp; no oversaturation
- Inclusive and diverse imagery; candid over staged
- Avoid cliché/studio stock unless intentional; careful consideration for alcohol/junk food
- No overlays or gradients on stock imagery

### Brand-Enhanced Imagery

- Stylised imagery specifically highlighting oOh! brand features (colours, environments, audiences)
- Used for key events, campaigns, and special presentations only

### Staff Photography

- Professional headshots: casual, friendly, premium style
- Employer brand photos in work environments — show what it's like to work at oOh!
- Internal comms: deep-etched imagery acceptable
- Playful and engaging — lower corporate hierarchy boundaries

### Photography Don'ts

1. Don't crop out assets or cover with colour blocks
2. Don't use cliché/staged audience photography — use candid, expressive faces
3. Don't deep-etch assets or use in collage treatment
4. Don't use cartoon-style illustrations (exceptions for specific events only)
5. Don't use flat deep-etched imagery on plain backgrounds — layer with circles or graphic devices
6. Photography on slides must always be full-bleed — no borders, no cropped frames
